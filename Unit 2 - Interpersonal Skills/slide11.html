Vocabulary & Figurative Language

Enhancing professional expression through the precise use of idioms, phrasal verbs, and collocations.

Idioms

Phrases where the figurative meaning is different from the literal meaning of its individual constituent parts.

Examples

"Break the ice"

To initiate a social interaction or conversation.

"Ball is in your court"

It is up to you to make the next decision.

Phrasal Verbs

A verb combined with a preposition or adverb (or both) that creates a meaning different from the original verb.

Examples

"Figure out"

To understand or solve a problem.

"Carry out"

To perform or complete a task/experiment.

Collocations

A series of words that naturally go together. Using them makes English sound natural and professional.

Examples

"Make a decision"

Not "do a decision".

"Strong coffee"

Not "powerful coffee".
