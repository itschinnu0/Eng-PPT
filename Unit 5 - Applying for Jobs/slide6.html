Unit 3: English for Employability

Grammar: Conditional Clauses

Structuring logical relationships between conditions and results (If... then...) to express facts, plans, and possibilities.

Zero Conditional

General Truths & Facts

Used for scientific facts, general truths, and instructions where the result is certain.

"If the system overheats, the alarm sounds."

If + Present Simple, Present Simple

First Conditional

Real Future Possibilities

Used for real situations that are likely to happen in the future (planning & predictions).

"If we finish the code today, we will deploy it tomorrow."

If + Present Simple, Will + Verb

Second Conditional

Hypothetical Situations

Used for unreal or imaginary situations in the present or future (unlikely scenarios).

"If we had a larger budget, we would upgrade the server."

If + Past Simple, Would + Verb

Third Conditional

Past Regrets & Reflection

Used for situations that did not happen in the past (post-mortem analysis).

"If we had tested earlier, we would have found the bug."

If + Past Perfect, Would Have + V3
