English for Employability

Grammar: Modal Verbs

Using auxiliary verbs to express ability, obligation, and possibility with precision in professional contexts.

Ability

Can / Could

Expresses capability or possibility. 'Can' is used for present ability; 'Could' implies past ability, polite requests, or hypothetical situations.

Examples

"The software can process big data."

"We could optimize the code later."

Capability & Polite Request

Obligation

Should / Must

Expresses advice and necessity. 'Should' offers a recommendation or suggestion; 'Must' indicates a mandatory requirement or strong obligation.

Examples

"You should review the report."

"Safety protocols must be followed."

Advice & Requirement

Possibility

May / Might

Expresses probability or formal permission. 'May' often suggests a slightly higher likelihood or formality than 'Might'.

Examples

"The project may start next week."

"We might need more resources."
