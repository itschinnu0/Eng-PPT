Tenses: Time of Action

Mastering verb tenses to accurately convey timeline, status, and planning in professional environments.

Past

Past Tense

Describes actions or states completed before the present moment. Essential for reporting results, documenting history, and reviewing milestones.

Completed Action

"The team finalized the design yesterday."

[Subject] + [Verb-ed/Irregular]

Pres

Present Tense

Indicates current actions, ongoing states, or general truths. Used for status updates, standard operating procedures, and real-time monitoring.

Routine / Fact

"The system monitors data in real-time."

[Subject] + [Verb / Verb-s]

Futr

Future Tense

Expresses actions expected to happen later. Critical for project proposals, scheduling upcoming tasks, and strategic planning.

Planned Action

"We will launch the beta next month."

[Subject] + will + [Verb]
