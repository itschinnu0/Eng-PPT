Voice: Active vs Passive

Choosing the Right Tone for Professional Communication

Active Voice

The subject performs the action. It is direct, concise, and clearly identifies responsibility.

Subject + Action: "The engineer fixed it."

Focus: The Actor/Agent

Tone: Engaging & Personal

Passive Voice

The subject receives the action. Used when the actor is unknown or the process is more important.

Action Received: "It was fixed."

Focus: The Action/Result

Tone: Objective & Formal

When to Use

Choose the right voice to match the document type and audience expectation.

Active: Emails, Instructions, Proposals

Passive: Lab Reports, Technical Specs

Tip: Don't overuse passive voice
