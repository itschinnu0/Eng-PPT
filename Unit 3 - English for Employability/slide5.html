Writing Skills

Reported Speech

Conveying information from original sources accurately while adapting tenses, pronouns, and time markers for professional documentation.

01

Core Concept

Reports the speaker's message without using their exact words. Essential for minutes of meetings and incident reports where direct quotes are not feasible.

Transformation

Direct: "We are testing the unit."

Reported: He said that they were testing the unit.

02

Tense Changes

Verbs typically shift one step back into the past (backshift) when the reporting verb is in the past tense. Present becomes Past; Past becomes Past Perfect.

Transformation

Direct: "The engineer has finished the design."

Reported: She said the engineer had finished the design.

03

Pronouns & Time

Pronouns change to reflect the reporter's perspective. Time and place markers shift (e.g., 'now' to 'then', 'tomorrow' to 'the next day').

Transformation

Direct: "I will submit it tomorrow."

Reported: He said he would submit it the next day.
