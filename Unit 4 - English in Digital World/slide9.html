Academic Integrity

Plagiarism & Infringement

Understanding the distinction between ethical violations and legal offenses, and implementing strategies to avoid them.

01

Plagiarism

The act of using someone else's ideas, words, or work without proper acknowledgment. It is fundamentally an ethical violation of trust.

Example

"Copying a paragraph from a report without quotation marks."

Theft of intellectual property

02

Infringement

Violating the exclusive rights of a copyright holder, such as unauthorized use or distribution. It is a legal violation punishable by law.

Example

"Distributing a paid textbook PDF to the entire team for free."

Violation of Copyright Law

03

How to Avoid

Maintain integrity by citing sources (APA/MLA), paraphrasing original text with new insights, and obtaining permissions for licensed material.

Solution

"According to Smith (2023), the data suggests..."
